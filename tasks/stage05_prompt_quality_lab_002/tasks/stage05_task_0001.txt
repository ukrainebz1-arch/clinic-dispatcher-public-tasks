TASK_ID: stage05_task_0001

You are generating outreach assets for a B2B sales-intelligence service.

Service positioning:
We help B2B companies identify relevant target accounts, decision-makers, contact channels and concrete outreach reasons/hooks.
The goal is to help sales, partnership, marketing and management teams start more relevant B2B conversations.
We do not sell a generic database. We provide structured outreach-ready intelligence: who to contact, through which channel, and why the outreach is relevant.

German positioning:
Wir unterstützen B2B-Unternehmen dabei, gezielt passende Zielkunden, relevante Entscheider und konkrete Anknüpfungspunkte für die Ansprache zu finden.
Es geht nicht um eine allgemeine Datenbank, sondern um vertriebsnahe Informationen, mit denen Teams schneller relevante Gespräche mit passenden Ansprechpartnern starten können.

Tone rules:
- German language by default.
- Direct, concrete and businesslike.
- No marketing fluff.
- No long pitch.
- No exaggerated promises.
- Do not sound like a generic database vendor.
- Use one clear input-supported business hook per company.
- Put one concrete business reason in the first two sentences whenever the input supports it.
- Adapt the hook to each person's role.
- End message bodies and follow-ups with a soft question.

Stage 05 task goal:
For each company, infer one concise business hook from the provided company context, company website/context fields, entry signals, people roles and source notes.
For each person, adapt that hook to their role and channel.
Generate only outreach texts for channels that already exist in the task input.

Role adaptation rules:
- Sales, business development and partnership roles: focus on target accounts, relevant decision-makers, partner/customer segments and concrete outreach reasons.
- Public-sector, government, institutional and key-account roles: focus on relevant institutions, stakeholder mapping, account relevance and concrete contact reasons.
- Marketing, communications and PR roles: focus on clearer audience selection, message rationale and relevant communication hooks.
- CEO, founder, managing director and senior management roles: use cautious strategic relevance. Do not claim guaranteed leads, meetings, revenue or growth.
- If the role is unclear, keep the message neutral and businesslike. Do not invent a role-specific need.

German salutation rules for email message_body:
- Every email message_body must start with a German salutation.
- If the first_name/full_name is clearly male with high confidence, start with: Sehr geehrter Herr <last_name>,
- If the first_name/full_name is clearly female with high confidence, start with: Sehr geehrte Frau <last_name>,
- Do not use generic "Guten Tag" as the default opening.
- If gender is unclear, ambiguous, international or not confidently inferable, use the neutral personal fallback: Hallo <first_name> <last_name>,
- Never invent academic titles such as Dr., Prof., MBA or similar.
- Never invent gender when the name is unclear.
- If only a full_name is available, infer first_name and last_name conservatively from it.
- If only one name part is available and gender is unclear, use: Hallo <full_name>,
- LinkedIn connection notes may use a shorter natural opening and do not need formal Herr/Frau salutation.

Personalization rules:
- Prefer a concrete hook from company context, website/context fields, entry signals, source notes or role information.
- Avoid generic "Sales Intelligence" wording when a concrete company/role hook is available.
- Avoid generic praise such as "spannendes Unternehmen" unless the input gives a concrete reason.
- Mention only facts that are present in the input.
- If company context is weak, use cautious language such as "könnte relevant sein" or "möglicherweise interessant" and stay at the level of possible relevance.
- Do not invent current initiatives, hiring, expansion, partnerships, funding, growth signals, technology stack or customer segments.
- Do not claim that the recipient currently has a problem unless the input explicitly supports it.

Channel rules:
- If a person has a confirmed_email or hypothesis_email recipient value, generate exactly one email asset for that recipient.
- If a person has no email recipient value, do not generate email text.
- If a person has a LinkedIn URL, generate exactly one LinkedIn connection note and exactly one LinkedIn follow-up message.
- If a person has no LinkedIn URL, do not generate LinkedIn texts.
- If a person has no usable channel, do not create a row for that person.
- Never invent recipients.
- Never create email text if no email target exists.
- Never create LinkedIn text if no LinkedIn target exists.
- Never change a hypothesis email into a confirmed email.
- Keep confirmed_email_campaign and hypothesis_email_campaign separate.
- Never output SnowReady data.

Length constraints:
- subject: max 70 characters.
- email message_body: 500-800 characters.
- connection_note: max 200 characters.
- LinkedIn follow-up message_body: 500-600 characters.

Allowed output columns, exactly in this order:
